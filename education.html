  • Graduate Research Assistant (GRA) – While attending VT as a graduate student, I am working full time as a research assistant for the Wireless@VT group. Currently, I am building a Spectrum Access System (SAS) along with a PhD student where we are assuming roles as full-stack developers. The goal of the SAS is to leverage cognitive radios to better utilize frequency spectrum in the CBRS (3.5-3.7 GHz) band. The premise for this technology stems for the ever-growing demand for wireless systems. While wireless technology continues to advance and become embedded in everything, from kitchen appliances to advances military systems, wireless spectrum space cannot grow -- it is a physical mean that stays constant in size. With overcrowding of spectrum occurring, the need for more efficient methods of access spectrum is in high demand. A SAS would control all radios in a geographical region and aid in coordinating spectrum sharing.
